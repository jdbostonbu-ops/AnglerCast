# Lake Whitefish (Coregonus clupeaformis)

Also known as: whitefish, common whitefish, eastern whitefish, Sault whitefish.

## What is a lake whitefish?

The lake whitefish is a silvery, deep-bodied freshwater fish in the whitefish subfamily of the salmon/trout family (Salmonidae), found in cold, deep lakes across Canada and the northern U.S., including the Great Lakes and northern New England. Like all salmonids it has a small adipose fin. A key feature is its small mouth set below a snout that overhangs the short lower jaw — an adaptation for feeding on the bottom. Lake whitefish are one of the most important commercial freshwater fish in North America, prized for their delicate, white flesh, and they're also caught recreationally, including through the ice.

## What does a lake whitefish eat?

Lake whitefish are mainly bottom feeders. Their overhanging snout and inferior mouth let them vacuum food off the lake bed — they eat aquatic insect larvae, small mollusks (including snails and small clams), crustaceans (like amphipods and small invertebrates), and fish eggs. They also rise to feed on emerging insects (mayflies, midges) at times, and zooplankton when young.

## When is a lake whitefish in season?

Lake whitefish are caught in cold months and are a popular ice-fishing target on northern lakes, as well as during late-fall spawning. Seasons and limits are set by state agencies and subject to change. Always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a lake whitefish spawn?

Lake whitefish spawn in late fall and early winter, generally moving into shallower water over clean, rocky or gravelly bottom (sometimes in open water) as temperatures drop. They broadcast their eggs with no nest and no parental care; the eggs settle into the bottom and incubate over winter, hatching in spring.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Freshwater. Lake whitefish live in cold, deep freshwater lakes.

## Fun fact / angler note

Lake whitefish are the backbone of the upper Great Lakes commercial fishery and are considered one of the finest-eating freshwater fish — mild, delicate, and especially prized smoked. Their downward-set mouth is a dead giveaway of a bottom feeder. Anglers increasingly target them through the ice, where they put up a surprisingly good fight on light tackle.
