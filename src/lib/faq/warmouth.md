# Warmouth (Lepomis gulosus)

Also known as: warmouth, goggle-eye, redeye, stumpknocker, mud bass.

## What is a warmouth?

The warmouth is a stocky, thick-bodied sunfish with a large mouth and red eyes, often mistaken for a rock bass or a small bass. It is dark and mottled — brassy to olive-brown with dark blotches and reddish-brown markings, and several dark lines radiating back from the eye. Warmouth like warm, weedy, mucky, slow or still waters: swamps, sluggish streams, ponds, and vegetated lake margins, often near stumps and woody cover. Their large mouth makes them more predatory than most sunfish, and they're an aggressive, fun catch where they occur.

## What does a warmouth eat?

Warmouth are more predatory than typical sunfish thanks to their big mouth. Their diet includes aquatic insects and larvae, crayfish, small crustaceans, snails, and a good deal of small fish — including minnows and the young of other species. They ambush prey from around cover like stumps and weed edges.

## When is a warmouth in season?

Warmouth are caught spring through fall and are aggressive, willing biters. They're usually managed under general panfish/sunfish rules set by state agencies and subject to change. Always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a warmouth spawn?

Warmouth spawn in late spring and summer, with the male fanning out a nest in the shallows (often near cover such as stumps, roots, or vegetation) and guarding the eggs and fry. Unlike the tight colonies of bluegill, warmouth often nest more solitarily. Survival of the young is variable.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Freshwater. Warmouth live in warm, weedy, slow or still freshwater — swamps, sluggish streams, ponds, and vegetated lake edges.

## Fun fact / angler note

The warmouth's nickname "goggle-eye" (shared with rock bass) comes from its big red eyes, and the dark lines radiating back from the eye are a good ID clue. With a mouth bigger than most sunfish, warmouth hit aggressively and pull hard, and they can take surprisingly large baits and lures for a panfish.
