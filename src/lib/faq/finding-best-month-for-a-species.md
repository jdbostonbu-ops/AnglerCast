
# Finding the Best Month for a Species

If you want to know when a specific species has historically been seen in your area, the Saltwater and Freshwater pages can tell you. You do not need to guess — the data answers the question directly.

## Zoom out on the map to see more records

The occurrence map under your search shows the actual record locations for the species and month you selected. By default the map starts zoomed in around your coordinates, which can hide records that are nearby but just outside the initial view. Zoom out on the map to see more occurrences across the wider area.

Zooming out is especially useful when the confidence is low — sometimes the data is thin in the immediate vicinity but stronger a short distance away. Seeing the broader pattern can tell you whether the species is actually rare in this region or just under-recorded right at your coordinates.

## The workflow

Open the Saltwater page if the species lives in the ocean, or the Freshwater page if it lives in rivers and lakes. Pick the species from the dropdown. Enter the coordinates of the area you want to fish — full precision, do not round. Pick a month, run the search, and read the sighting rate that comes back.

To find the best month, run the same search a few times with different months. The month with the highest sighting rate — and a confidence flag of moderate or high — is the historically strongest month for that species in that area. Write down the rates as you go so you can compare them side by side.

## Read the confidence, not just the percentage

A 50% sighting rate based on 8 records is not a strong signal. A 25% sighting rate based on 800 records is. AnglerCast always shows the sample size and a confidence flag for this reason. When the confidence is low, the data is too thin — treat the month as a maybe, not an answer.

If every month for your species and area shows low confidence, the species may be rare in that region or under-recorded. Try widening the search to a nearby spot with more data.

## What the result means

The best month is the month where the species has historically been recorded most often in your area. It is not a guarantee that fish will be there this year, and it is not a substitute for checking conditions before you go. It tells you when to plan around — not whether the day will be productive.

For day-to-day decisions, use the Explore page to check the live conditions and combine that with the historical pattern from this search.
