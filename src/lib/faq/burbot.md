# Burbot (Lota lota)

Also known as: burbot, ling, lawyer, eelpout, freshwater cod, cusk.

## What is a burbot?

The burbot is a strange and distinctive fish — the only member of the cod family that lives entirely in fresh water. It has a long, eel-like rear body, a single chin barbel (like its marine cod relatives), mottled brown-and-yellow marbled skin, and a broad head. Burbot live in cold, deep lakes and rivers across the northern U.S. and Canada, staying in deep, cold water most of the year. They're most active and accessible in winter, when they uniquely move shallow to spawn under the ice — making them a prized and unusual ice-fishing target. Their firm, white flesh is excellent eating, earning them the nickname "poor man's lobster."

## What does a burbot eat?

Burbot are aggressive cold-water predators that feed mostly at night and in cold water. They eat fish (including ciscoes, sculpins, young perch, and others), along with crayfish, insects, mollusks, and fish eggs. They're effective deep-water and under-ice hunters, using their chin barbel and keen senses to find prey in dark, cold conditions.

## When is a burbot in season?

Burbot are caught mainly in winter — they're one of the few fish that bite best in the coldest months, especially around their midwinter spawn, which makes them a favorite of dedicated ice anglers. Seasons and limits are set by state agencies and subject to change. Always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a burbot spawn?

Burbot are remarkable for spawning in the dead of winter, under the ice, usually January–February. They gather in shallow water over sand or gravel and spawn at night, sometimes forming a writhing, ball-like spawning aggregation. They broadcast huge numbers of tiny eggs with no nest and no parental care; the eggs drift and settle to incubate in the cold water.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Freshwater. Burbot are the only freshwater member of the cod family, living in cold, deep freshwater lakes and rivers.

## Fun fact / angler note

The burbot's nicknames tell the story: "freshwater cod" (it really is a true cod), "eelpout" (for its eel-like tail), and "poor man's lobster" (for its sweet, firm, lobster-like flesh when cooked). It's one of the only fish that spawns under winter ice, which is why "eelpout" ice-fishing has become a celebrated cold-weather tradition in parts of the North. Don't be put off by the slithery look — burbot are outstanding eating.
