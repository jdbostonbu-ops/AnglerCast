# Bluefish (Pomatomus saltatrix)

Also known as: tailor, snapper blue, baby blues, choppers, elfs.

## What is a bluefish?

The bluefish is a fast, aggressive saltwater predator found along the U.S. Atlantic coast from Maine to Florida, and more broadly from Nova Scotia to Argentina. It is a migratory, warm-temperate species that travels in schools of similar-sized fish, moving into the Middle Atlantic region in spring and heading south or farther offshore in fall. Bluefish grow quickly, can reach more than three feet long and over 25 pounds, and may live to age 12 or more. They are one of the most popular recreational fish on the Atlantic coast.

## What does a bluefish eat?

Bluefish are apex piscivores — fish-eaters — and are known for their voracious, slashing feeding behavior. They feed primarily on schooling coastal fish such as menhaden, herring, and other small baitfish, and supplement that with squid, shrimp, and crabs. Their diet shifts with their size, location, and the season, driven largely by what prey is locally abundant. Their aggressive feeding is what gives them nicknames like "choppers."

## When is a bluefish in season?

Along the Northeast coast, bluefish are an inshore summer and early-fall presence, generally arriving as waters warm in spring and moving back south or offshore as waters cool in fall. Exact recreational seasons and bag limits are set by state and federal regulators and change from year to year, so always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a bluefish spawn?

Bluefish spawn offshore in oceanic waters, not in estuaries. There are multiple spawning peaks across the range: spawning begins in late spring south of Cape Hatteras, North Carolina (with a peak roughly March–May near the continental shelf edge), and a second peak occurs over the Mid-Atlantic continental shelf from about June through August. Eggs and larvae develop in open ocean water, and juveniles later move into bays and estuaries, which serve as nursery grounds. Bluefish typically reach maturity by about age 2.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Saltwater. Bluefish live in coastal ocean waters, large bays, and estuaries across the continental shelf. Juveniles use estuaries as nursery habitat, but the species is marine and spawns in the open ocean.

## Fun fact / angler note

Bluefish are famous for "blitzes" — feeding frenzies where a school corrals baitfish against the surface or beach and attacks in a churning froth, sometimes close enough to shore that surfcasters can reach them. Anglers handle them carefully: bluefish have sharp teeth and a strong bite, so a wire or heavy leader and a careful hand at unhooking are standard practice.
