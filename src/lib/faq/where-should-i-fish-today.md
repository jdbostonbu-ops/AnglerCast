# Where Should I Fish Today

There is no single right answer — the best spot today depends on what you are targeting, where the conditions look favorable, and how the historical record lines up with the time of year. AnglerCast gives you the pieces to answer it for yourself.

## Start with the month and the species

Decide what you want to catch. Open the Saltwater or Freshwater page for that species, pick the current month, and try a few candidate locations. The sighting rate plus the confidence flag tells you which areas have historically produced this species in this month. Compare a few spots side by side and shortlist the ones with the strongest historical pattern.

If you do not have a target species in mind, pick two or three you would be happy to catch and run them all. A spot that looks decent across several species is often a better day on the water than a spot that peaks for one.

## Check the conditions on the Explore page

For each shortlisted spot, go to the Explore page and enter your launch point and the destination coordinates. AnglerCast pulls live wind, wave, swell, current, and water-temperature data for that destination, plus tide predictions for saltwater locations. The result tells you what the boat ride looks like and what conditions you would be fishing in when you arrive.

A spot with a great historical pattern but a bad weather day is not the right spot today. A spot with a moderate pattern but ideal conditions often is.

## Combine the two signals

The historical pattern (from the species search) tells you whether fish have been there in this month before. The live conditions (from the Explore page) tell you whether today is a fishable day there. The best spot today is the intersection of both — a place where the records say the species shows up in this month and the conditions say the water is workable.

If nothing lines up well, the honest answer might be to wait for a better day or pick a different species. AnglerCast will not invent confidence where the data does not support it.

## Zoom out and explore

If your shortlisted spots all show low confidence or weak conditions, zoom out on the occurrence map to see what is happening in the wider area. Sometimes a slightly different put-in or a nearby cove changes the picture. Real fishing is judgment, not just math — AnglerCast gives you the numbers; you make the call.
