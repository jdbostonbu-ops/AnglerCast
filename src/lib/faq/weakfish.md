# Weakfish (Cynoscion regalis)

Also known as: squeteague, sea trout, gray trout, tiderunner, common seatrout.

## What is a weakfish?

The weakfish is a sleek, trout-shaped member of the drum family (Sciaenidae), found in the western Atlantic from Nova Scotia to Florida and most abundant from New York to North Carolina. It is silvery with a greenish or olive back, often with a golden or purple sheen and fine dark speckling, and two prominent canine teeth in the upper jaw. The name "weakfish" refers not to the fight but to the tender, easily torn membrane in the mouth, which can pull free of a hook. Weakfish are a coastal and estuarine schooling species.

## What does a weakfish eat?

Weakfish are opportunistic predators whose diet changes as they grow. Juveniles eat mostly invertebrates such as mysid and other shrimp; older fish shift to a fish-heavy diet, especially small schooling fish like anchovies, menhaden, silversides, and spot, along with crabs, shrimp, squid, and mollusks. They forage throughout the water column and adapt to locally available prey.

## When is a weakfish in season?

Weakfish are a warm-season inshore presence in the Northeast — they migrate north in spring, spend summer in bays, estuaries, and coastal waters, and move south and offshore in late fall. Seasons, size limits, and bag limits are set by the Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission and individual states and change from year to year. Always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a weakfish spawn?

Weakfish spawn in nearshore coastal waters, bays, and estuaries — often near larger inlets — from about May through August (beginning earlier in the south), with peak activity in late spring. The eggs are pelagic and hatch in just 2–3 days. Larvae and juveniles use low-salinity estuarine waters as nursery grounds. Weakfish return to the same spawning areas each year, and older, larger females produce more eggs and more batches per season. Most mature by around age 1.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Saltwater. Weakfish are a coastal marine and estuarine species over sand and mud bottoms; juveniles depend on estuarine nurseries and occasionally venture into fresher water, but the species is marine.

## Fun fact / angler note

Handle weakfish gently at the boat — the soft mouth tissue that gives the fish its name tears easily, so a hook can pull free with too much pressure. They're prized as both a sporting and table fish, and a big "tiderunner" is a well-earned catch.
