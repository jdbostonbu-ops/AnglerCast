# Black Sea Bass (Centropristis striata)

Also known as: sea bass, blackfish, black will, rockbass, humpback (large males).

## What is a black sea bass?

The black sea bass is a temperate reef fish found in the western Atlantic from Cape Cod (sometimes farther north) to Florida and into the Gulf of Mexico, concentrated from Massachusetts to Cape Canaveral. It is a structure-loving bottom fish that lives around rocky bottoms, reefs, wrecks, jetties, and pilings. Smoky gray to blackish in color, larger males often develop a humped back and a blue tint around the eyes during spawning season. The northern stock migrates seasonally — inshore in shallow water in summer, offshore to deeper water in winter.

## What does a black sea bass eat?

Black sea bass are opportunistic predators that eat whatever is available, using a large mouth to grab prey near structure. Their diet favors crabs, shrimp, worms, clams, mussels, and small fish. They feed close to the bottom around the reefs and wrecks they inhabit.

## When is a black sea bass in season?

Black sea bass are an inshore summer presence in the Northeast, arriving in shallow coastal waters as it warms and moving offshore as it cools. Seasons, size limits, and bag limits are set by the Atlantic States Marine Fisheries Commission and individual states and change from year to year. Always check current local regulations before keeping fish.

## When does a black sea bass spawn?

Black sea bass are protogynous hermaphrodites — most begin life as females and later change sex to become males, generally between ages 2 and 5 (about 9–13 inches). As a result, most small fish are female and most large fish are male. Spawning occurs inshore over the continental shelf; in the northern stock it runs roughly from April through October, peaking in summer, while southern fish spawn earlier. During spawning, a male gathers and aggressively defends a group of females. Eggs and larvae are pelagic, and juveniles settle into structurally complex nearshore and estuarine habitats.

## Saltwater or freshwater?

Saltwater. Black sea bass are a marine reef-associated species living on the continental shelf and nearshore structure. Juveniles use estuaries, but the species is fully marine.

## Fun fact / angler note

Nearly every trophy-sized black sea bass is a male — because the species starts out female and switches sex with age and size, the biggest "humpback" fish are old males. They hold tight to structure, so anglers target them over wrecks, reefs, and rock piles.
